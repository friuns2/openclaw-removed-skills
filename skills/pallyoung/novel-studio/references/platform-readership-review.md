# Platform Readership Review

## 1. Goal

Evaluate fiction not only as a piece of writing, but as a serial reading product for web-fiction platforms.

Use this reference when the user asks about:
- whether the opening is strong enough
- whether the work has chase-read / binge potential
- whether the story feels commercially readable
- whether the pacing, hook density, or payoff style fits platform expectations

---

## 2. Core principle

A technically correct story can still fail as platform fiction.

Platform readability depends on factors such as:
- opening capture strength
- chapter-end pull
- payoff frequency
- emotional and爽点 cadence
- protagonist attraction sustainability
- low-friction readability

Do not confuse “well organized” with “highly readable.”

---

## 3. Main review dimensions

### 3.1 Opening capture strength
Check:
- does the first 1-3 chapters create immediate curiosity, tension, or desire?
- is the protagonist situation vivid quickly?
- is there a strong reading promise early enough?

### 3.2 Chase-read pull
Check:
- do chapter endings naturally pull the reader onward?
- are questions, costs, or emotional tensions left active at the end?
- does the next chapter feel needed, not optional?

### 3.3 Payoff density
Check:
- are there enough mini-payoffs, reveals, reversals, wins, emotional breakthroughs, or face-slaps?
- does the story wait too long before rewarding reader patience?

### 3.4 Protagonist addiction value
Check:
- is the protagonist someone readers want to keep following?
- does the protagonist repeatedly create curiosity, admiration, desire, fear, or emotional attachment?

### 3.5 Platform friction
Check:
- is the prose easy to follow at reading speed?
- does exposition drag?
- are scenes too soft, too abstract, or too slow for serialized consumption?

### 3.6 Update-sustainability feel
Check:
- does the structure look capable of sustaining repeated chapter hooks and payoffs?
- will the story likely sag after the opening promise?

---

## 4. Common commercial-readability failures

### 4.1 Slow but not rich
The opening is not gripping enough, and the slow pace does not compensate with atmosphere, mystery, or emotional depth.

### 4.2 Correct but not addictive
The story is coherent, but nothing compels continued reading.

### 4.3 Hook delay
The real attractive engine starts too late.

### 4.4 Payoff starvation
Pressure accumulates, but release comes too rarely.

### 4.5 Protagonist competence without fascination
The protagonist functions well but lacks reader obsession value.

---

## 5. Practical workflow use

During discovery / planning:
- test whether the concept has platform-level hook and chase-read promise

During drafting review:
- test whether chapters end with enough continuation pull
- test whether payoff cadence is too sparse

During proofreading / evaluation:
- explicitly separate “good craft” from “good serialized readability”
