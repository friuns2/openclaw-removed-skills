# Scoring Rubric

## 1. Goal

Provide a repeatable scoring framework for evaluating outlines, character packages, chapter batches, and proofreading outcomes.

Use this rubric when the user asks for:
- quality evaluation
- readiness judgment
- chapter or batch review
- outline strength review
- whether something is good enough to proceed

Do not use scores as decoration. Use them to surface what is weak, what is strong, and what must be repaired before advancement.

---

## 2. General scoring scale

Use a 10-point scale for each dimension.

- 9-10: strong, clearly effective, worth preserving
- 7-8: usable and solid, but still improvable
- 5-6: technically passable, but noticeably weak or generic
- 3-4: structurally problematic or creatively flat
- 1-2: unusable for current stage

Always accompany scores with reasons.

---

## 3. Outline scoring dimensions

### 3.1 Hook strength
Does the concept generate immediate reader curiosity or desire?

### 3.2 Main-line drive
Is the main plot goal clear, escalating, and sustainable?

### 3.3 Side-line integration
Do relationship lines, suspense lines, and side plots meaningfully pressure the main line?

### 3.4 Arc escalation
Do turning points and payoff paths visibly intensify?

### 3.5 Long-form sustainability
Can this structure carry many chapters without obvious collapse?

### 3.6 Market readability / readability promise
Does the concept feel readable, attractive, and platform-appropriate?

---

## 4. Character-package scoring dimensions

### 4.1 Protagonist attraction
Is the protagonist memorable beyond labels?

### 4.2 Core-cast differentiation
Do important characters feel distinct in motive, rhythm, and dramatic role?

### 4.3 Relationship tension
Do key relationships naturally generate scenes, pressure, and emotional movement?

### 4.4 Character contradiction and cost
Do major roles have usable contradiction, weakness, price, or hidden pressure?

### 4.5 Scene usability
Can the current role package support actual scenes, not just profile summaries?

---

## 5. Chapter / batch scoring dimensions

### 5.1 Dramatic movement
Does something meaningfully change in conflict, cost, emotion, relationship, or knowledge?

### 5.2 Hook / continuation pull
Does the chapter create desire to keep reading?

### 5.3 Character liveliness
Do characters feel alive on the page, not just functional?

### 5.4 Plot effectiveness
Does the chapter advance or intensify important lines rather than just transport the cast?

### 5.5 Language effectiveness
Does the prose support pace, tone, and scene impact appropriately?

### 5.6 Emotional effect
Does the chapter produce the intended emotional charge?

---

## 6. Proofreading / review scoring dimensions

### 6.1 Continuity integrity
### 6.2 Logic integrity
### 6.3 OOC risk control
### 6.4 Side-line payoff quality
### 6.5 Character-attraction retention
### 6.6 Revision priority clarity
### 6.7 Platform readability / chase-read pull
### 6.8 Long-form sustainability confidence

---

## 7. Output pattern

When scoring, provide:
- dimension scores
- strongest dimensions
- weakest dimensions
- pass / revise / rollback recommendation
- top 1-3 repair priorities

Keep the score report compact by default.
Only expand into long explanation when the user explicitly asks for more detail.

Avoid fake precision. The score is a decision aid, not a mask for vague comments.
