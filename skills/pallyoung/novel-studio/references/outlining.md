# Outlining

## 1. Goal

Turn a confirmed project direction into a story skeleton strong enough to support drafting.

This stage bridges:
- topic selection
- story identity
- structural planning
- long-form chapter production

---

## 2. Required input

You must have:
- confirmed final title or explicitly approved working title
- a usable topic report
- a usable idea expansion package

Before starting planning, prioritize these sections from `00_选题报告.md`:
- 最终推荐题材
- 最终标题 / working title
- 一句话核心钩子
- 项目方向结论
- 风险提醒
- Cliche Exhaustion Loop retained novelty axes

Treat these as planning anchors, not optional background notes.

---

## 3. Forbidden to start if

Do not start outlining if:
- title confirmation is incomplete
- the story hook is still generic
- the protagonist situation is still undefined
- the project still lacks a stable concept package

---

## 4. Supporting craft references

When outlining, also use these references as needed:
- read `plot-weaving.md` when designing main plot / side plot / relationship-line interweaving
- read `narrative-techniques.md` when choosing escalation shape, reversal timing, chapter-ending engines, and arc-level dramatic patterns

## 5. Planning clarification loop

Before formal outline generation, run a planning clarification loop with the user.

This loop must clarify, as needed:
- story intensity
- pacing preference
- romance / relationship weight
- worldbuilding scale
- protagonist growth style
- conflict style
- opening-stage emotional direction
- opening-stage plot direction
- long-form expansion expectation

Do not generate the formal outline until these planning-level preferences are usable enough to avoid blind structural guesswork.

If the user’s preference is still unstable, remain in clarification and continue discussing.

Before planning approval, the retained direction must also complete the supervisor-side deep anti-cliche pass:
- cliché samples were enumerated
- retained novelty axes were named explicitly
- the outline states what default genre rail it is refusing to fall back into
- when the supervisor activates that pass, the retained conclusion is persisted in `05_定稿结论.md`

---

## 6. Required outline elements

A usable outline must include:
- main plot line
- protagonist progression path
- major arcs or volumes
- at least three major turning points
- major suspense or climax nodes
- conflict escalation path
- early chapter guidance
- long-form sustainability logic

---

## 7. Required output

This stage must produce:
- `01_想法.md`
- `02_大纲.md`
- optionally `04_章节骨架.md`

The output must not remain only in chat.

---

## 8. Completion standard

This stage is complete only if:
- the concept package clearly defines hook, protagonist setup, conflict, and story promise
- planning clarification has been completed to a usable level
- the outline contains at least three major turning points
- escalation path is visible
- early chapter direction exists
- the retained direction has passed deep anti-cliche review with named novelty axes
- any activated deep anti-cliche pass has a persisted `05_定稿结论.md` conclusion
- important side plots have explicit function and feedback paths into the main line
- the line structure does not rely on a single flat story rail
- the user explicitly approves the planning result

---

## 9. Do not advance if

Do not advance to character bible or drafting if:
- the outline is still summary-only
- major conflict progression is missing
- suspense or climax logic is absent
- early chapter direction is absent
- planning clarification is still incomplete
- cliché samples were not enumerated for the retained direction
- the supervisor activated deep anti-cliche review but no `05_定稿结论.md` records the retained conclusion
- the user has unresolved objections
- user approval to advance is missing

---

## 10. Hard drafting gate

Do not enter drafting without a usable outline.

A usable outline means:
- not just premise
- not just world notes
- not just theme statements
- not just a rough arc name list

It must be structurally actionable.

---

## 11. Rollback condition

Return to outlining if:
- drafting reveals structural weakness
- escalation collapses
- key payoff is unsupported
- the opening chapters cannot be written cleanly from the current outline

Return to the planning clarification loop if:
- the user says the direction is still not the right flavor
- pacing / tone / conflict style is still not aligned
- the generated outline feels technically valid but creatively off-target

---

## 12. Quality bar

A valid outline is:
- structural
- specific
- escalation-aware
- chapter-launchable
- sustainable for long-form work
- aligned with clarified user preference

Invalid output includes:
- vague summaries
- no meaningful turning points
- no opening chapter guidance
- no visible conflict engine
- no planning clarification before structural generation
