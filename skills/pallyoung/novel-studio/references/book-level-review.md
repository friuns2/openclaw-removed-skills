# Book-Level Review

## 1. Goal

Evaluate a novel project at whole-book scale rather than only chapter or batch scale.

Use this reference when the user asks about:
- overall project quality
- long-form sustainability
- whether the book will sag later
- whether the current structure can support serialization at scale
- whether the whole project is ready for major continuation or delivery

---

## 2. Core principle

A chapter can work while the book still fails.
A batch can feel strong while the long-form engine is weakening.

Book-level review must look beyond local success and ask:
- does the whole machine keep working?
- will attraction decay?
- will lines converge or sprawl?
- will momentum survive scale?

---

## 3. Book-level review dimensions

### 3.1 Opening promise vs later support
Does the later structure truly support the promise made by the opening?

### 3.2 Mid-story sustainability
Will the book still have enough tension sources, variation, and payoff paths after the initial novelty fades?

### 3.3 Main-line endurance
Can the main plot continue to intensify, or does it peak too early?

### 3.4 Side-line lifespan
Do side plots enrich the book over time, or do they sprawl, stall, and consume energy?

### 3.5 Protagonist attraction decay risk
Will the protagonist remain interesting after many chapters, or is charm too dependent on one early gimmick?

### 3.6 Relationship-line sustainability
Do key relationships have stages, reversals, and evolution, or will they flatten out quickly?

### 3.7 Payoff distribution
Are payoffs distributed across the book, or are they front-loaded / back-loaded in a way that harms retention?

### 3.8 Structural collapse risk
Where is the most likely future collapse point?
Examples:
- after the first major victory
- after the identity reveal
- after the romance line stabilizes
- after revenge is partially achieved

---

## 4. Book-level warning signs

### 4.1 Front-loaded excitement
The opening is strong, but later arcs have no stronger engine.

### 4.2 Endless branch growth
More lines open than close.

### 4.3 Protagonist stagnation
The protagonist keeps winning but stops transforming, surprising, or deepening.

### 4.4 Relationship exhaustion
The emotional line repeats the same beat without stage change.

### 4.5 Payoff drift
Promised payoff keeps being delayed or softened.

---

## 5. Output pattern

When using book-level review, report:
- overall health judgment
- strongest long-form asset
- biggest long-form risk
- likely collapse point
- what to reinforce before continuing large-scale expansion

---

## 6. Practical workflow use

Use this review:
- after a major outline is completed
- after several drafting batches exist
- before large continuation
- before final delivery if the project is intended as a serious long-form product
