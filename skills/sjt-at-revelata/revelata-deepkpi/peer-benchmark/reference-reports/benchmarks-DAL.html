Diff Insight
What the benchmark set reveals

On the airline segment, DAL generates 3.0¢ more TRASM than AAL and 3.4¢ more than UAL on essentially identical ASM bases — a revenue quality premium that more than explains the higher CASM. The benchmark set confirms this is a revenue story, not a cost story: DAL's 21.26¢ TRASM produces a spread peers can't match.

The refinery diff surfaces the more analytically interesting question. Merchant refiner margins of $14.60/bbl (PARR) and $13.64/bbl (CVI) represent what Monroe could earn as a standalone profit-maximizing refiner. Monroe reported only a −$0.04/gal fuel cost benefit to the airline in FY2025. DAL is forgoing ~$13–15/bbl in merchant margin in exchange for supply certainty and jet fuel price volatility reduction.

Notes
  • ALK TRASM gap is partly metric mismatch — ALK reports PRASM (passenger revenue only) vs. DAL's TRASM (total). The 13.81¢ PRASM gap to DAL's 21.26¢ TRASM overstates the true spread.
  • LUV load factor of 77.4% sourced from pvid=10559 — same pvid returned for AAL in prior queries; verify independently against LUV's 10-K disclosure.
  • Monroe's refining economics are not disclosed as standalone P&L. The −$0.04/gal fuel impact is the only reported metric; comparing directly to merchant refiner margins understates the hedging value.
