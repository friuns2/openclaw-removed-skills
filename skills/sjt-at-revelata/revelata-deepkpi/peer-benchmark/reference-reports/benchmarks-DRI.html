What the benchmark set reveals

The grid exposes four very different performance stories within the same portfolio. LongHorn is the standout — +5.1% comps nearly matching TXRH's +4.9%, yet at a $5.2M AUV vs. TXRH's $8.7M, suggesting significant pricing/throughput headroom if LH can be repositioned upmarket. Olive Garden's +1.7% tracks loosely with Cracker Barrel's value-casual comp baseline (~+3%), confirming a saturated value-casual category dynamic. The near-zero Other Business comps (+0.2%) reflect a blended partial-year Chuy's drag — no benchmark column is populated because the segment is a mixed bag with no clean analog.

Fine Dining's −3.0% comps are the structural gap: every benchmark column is blank because no standalone public fine-dining comp exists after RUTH was absorbed in October 2023. Only CAKE's ~$11-12M AUV provides a loose reference for DRI's $7.2M FD AUV — the $4-5M gap between them suggests DRI's fine-dining properties operate below the scale-premium threshold that makes large-format upscale restaurants defensible. The blank row visualization is not a data problem; it's the insight.

Notes
  • Fine Dining gap: RUTH was the closest standalone fine-dining comp, absorbed into DRI (Oct 2023). No S&P 1500 standalone exists. FD AUV $7.2M, comps −3.0% can only be benchmarked against DRI's own history.
  • BLMN — zero Revelata KPI coverage: Bloomin' (Outback/Fleming's) is structurally the closest whole-company comp but returned no KPIs. Pull BLMN filings directly for numeric comparison.
  • EAT comps outlier context: Chili's FY2025 +25.0% is a turnaround year, not a steady-state benchmark. Pre-FY2019 Brinker comp values encode basis-point integers and are unreliable.
  • DRI FY runs June–May: FY ends ~May 25. TXRH ends December; EAT ends June; CAKE ends December; CBRL ends late July. Period-adjust when comparing multi-year comp trajectories.
  • Other Business Chuy's effect: Chuy's (acquired Oct 2024) entered Other Business mid-FY2025 with a different AUV and cost structure. The $5.8M blended AUV and +0.2% comps reflect a partial-year mix; no clean benchmarks exist for this composite segment.
