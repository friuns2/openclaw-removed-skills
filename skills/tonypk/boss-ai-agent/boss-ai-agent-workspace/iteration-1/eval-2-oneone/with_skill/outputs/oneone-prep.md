**Goal**: Build warmth and psychological safety. Do NOT jump into the missed check-ins.

**Body language / tone cues**:

- Match their energy -- if they seem tired or low, be gentle

## Phase 3: Gentle Check-In (5-7 minutes)

**Goal**: Understand what is going on without making Timi feel accused.

**DO NOT say**:
- "You haven't submitted any check-ins"
- "Your submission rate is 0%"
- "You've missed 7 days in a row"
- "Is there a problem with your performance?"

**DO say** (pick the approach that fits your style):

**Approach A -- Indirect care framing**:
> "I noticed I haven't heard from you in a while on the daily updates. I just want to make sure everything is okay on your end. No pressure at all -- I'm asking because I care about how you're doing."

**Approach B -- System/process framing**:
> "I've been thinking about our check-in process and whether it's working well for everyone. How has it been for you? Is there anything about it that feels like it doesn't fit your workflow?"

**Approach C -- Team framing**:
> "We've been trying to keep everyone connected through the daily updates. I know it can feel like extra work sometimes. How are you feeling about things in general?"

**Follow-up prompts** (use only if Timi opens up):
- "Is there anything I can do to help?"
- "Are there any blockers or challenges you're dealing with that I should know about?"
- "Sometimes things get overwhelming and that's completely normal. What would make things easier for you?"

**If Timi is quiet or deflects**: Do not push. Filipino culture values hiya (a sense of shame/propriety) -- pushing too hard will cause withdrawal, not openness. Instead, say:
> "No worries at all. I just want you to know that if something comes up, you can always reach out to me. There's no judgment here."

## Phase 4: Listen and Diagnose (5-10 minutes)

**Goal**: Let Timi talk. Your job is to listen, not to solve.

**Possible root causes to listen for**:

| Signal | What Timi might say | What it might mean |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Personal issues | "Family stuff" / goes quiet | Health, family emergency, financial stress |
| Disengagement | "I've been busy" / vague answers | May be losing motivation or looking elsewhere |
| Process friction | "I forget" / "It's hard to do daily" | Check-in format or timing doesn't work for them |
| Overwhelm | "Too many things going on" | Workload issue, needs task prioritization help |
| Conflict | Avoids certain topics | Possible team friction or management concern |
| Technical | "I didn't get the messages" | Channel/bot issue, notification settings |

**Listening techniques**:
- Nod and use affirming phrases: "I understand", "That makes sense", "Thank you for sharing that"
- Reflect back: "It sounds like [paraphrase]. Is that right?"
- Do NOT interrupt or offer solutions immediately
- Silence is okay -- give Timi space to think

## Phase 5: Collaborative Solutions (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Co-create a plan that Timi feels ownership over. Do NOT impose solutions.

**Frame it as partnership, not directive**:

> "What do you think would help? I'm open to adjusting things if that makes it easier."

**Possible adjustments to offer**:

1. **Timing**: "Would a different time for check-ins work better for you? Maybe end of day instead of morning?"
2. **Format**: "Would you prefer a shorter format? Even just one sentence is fine."
3. **Channel**: "Is Telegram the best way to reach you, or would you prefer something else?"
4. **Frequency**: "If daily feels like too much, we could try every other day to start."
5. **Support**: "If you're dealing with a lot right now, I can help re-prioritize your tasks."

**Important**: Whatever Timi agrees to, keep it small and achievable. The goal is to rebuild the habit, not to get perfect compliance overnight.
