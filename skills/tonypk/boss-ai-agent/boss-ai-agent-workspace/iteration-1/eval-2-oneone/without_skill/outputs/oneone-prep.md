## Possible Reasons to Consider

Before the conversation, keep an open mind. Common reasons for prolonged disengagement in a Philippines-based context include:

- **Family obligations** -- Extended family responsibilities are deeply valued in Filipino culture. A family emergency (illness, typhoon damage, death in the family) may have taken priority.
- **Health concerns** -- Physical or mental health issues that Timi may feel uncomfortable disclosing.
- **Connectivity or infrastructure issues** -- Internet outages, power interruptions, or device problems are common, especially outside Metro Manila.
- **Workload or burnout** -- Feeling overwhelmed and silently withdrawing rather than raising it (a pattern known as "tahimik na lang" -- staying quiet to avoid conflict).
- **Unclear expectations** -- Timi may not fully understand the importance of the check-in process, or may feel the questions are not relevant to his work.
- **Personal dissatisfaction** -- Issues with the role, compensation, or team dynamics that Timi has not felt safe to raise.

## Cultural Considerations (Philippines)

Filipino workplace culture has several important nuances to keep in mind:

1. **"Hiya" (Sense of Shame/Social Propriety):** Timi may feel embarrassed about the missed check-ins and avoid the conversation. Approaching with blame will make him shut down further. Frame the conversation as concern, not discipline.

2. **Indirect Communication:** Filipinos often communicate indirectly, especially with authority figures. Timi may say "okay lang po" (I am okay) even if he is not. Watch for what is *not* said as much as what is said.

3. **Respect for Authority ("Po" and "Opo"):** Timi will likely be deferential. This means he may agree with everything you say without truly expressing his perspective. Create space for his real feelings.

4. **"Pakikisama" (Smooth Interpersonal Relations):** He may avoid conflict or disagreement. Make it explicit that honest feedback is welcome and safe.

5. **"Utang na Loob" (Debt of Gratitude):** If you approach with genuine kindness, Timi will likely reciprocate with loyalty and effort. This is a relationship-building opportunity.

## Recommended Conversation Approach

### Opening (5 minutes) -- Build Safety

Start with warmth. Do NOT open with the missed check-ins.

- "Kumusta ka, Timi? How have you been?"
- "I wanted to catch up with you -- it has been a while since we connected."
- Share something lighthearted or personal first (weekend plans, a recent team win).

**Goal:** Signal that this is a supportive conversation, not a disciplinary one.

### Discovery (10 minutes) -- Understand the Situation

Transition gently into the topic. Use open-ended questions:

- "I noticed we have not heard from you in the daily check-ins recently. I just wanted to make sure everything is okay on your end."
- "Is there anything going on that has been making it hard to do the check-ins?"
- "How are things going with your work generally? Anything feeling stuck or unclear?"

**Listen actively.** If Timi says "okay lang po," gently probe:

- "I appreciate that -- but I want to make sure. Sometimes things pile up and it is hard to say. Is there anything at all I can help with?"

**Do NOT:**
- Accuse or blame ("You missed 7 days of check-ins.")
- Frame it as a performance issue right away.
- Compare him to other team members.

### Problem-Solving (10 minutes) -- Collaborate on a Path Forward

Once you understand the root cause, work together on next steps:

- **If personal/family issue:** "Take the time you need. Let us figure out a check-in cadence that works for you right now. Even a quick one-liner is fine."
- **If connectivity issue:** "Let us see if there is a way to make the check-in easier -- maybe a different time of day or a simpler format."
- **If burnout/workload:** "Let us look at what is on your plate together. I would rather adjust priorities than have you burning out silently."
- **If unclear on process:** "The check-ins are not about monitoring you -- they are so I can support you better. Let me explain what I am looking for."
- **If disengagement/dissatisfaction:** "Your perspective matters to me. If something is not working, I genuinely want to know so we can fix it together."

### Closing (5 minutes) -- Reaffirm and Agree

- Summarize what was discussed and agreed upon.
- Reaffirm your support: "I am glad we talked. I am here to help, not to add pressure."
- Set a concrete, small next step: "Can we try just a short check-in tomorrow? Even two sentences is great."
- End positively: "Salamat, Timi. I appreciate you taking the time to talk."
